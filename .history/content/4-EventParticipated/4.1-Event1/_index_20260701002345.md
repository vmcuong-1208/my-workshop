# Summary Report: “AWS FIRST CLOUD AI JOURNEY COMMUNITY DAY 23/05/2026”

## 1. Context Is Everything" — Making AI Actually Work for You

This talk is part of a series of technology-sharing events organized by Amazon Web Services (AWS), focusing on the topic of Context Engineering — the art of providing the right context to maximize the power of AI models.

### Speakers

- **Tinh Truong** – Platform Engineer tại GoTymeX.

### Summary of content and main activities

The talk revolved around the core message: "Context Is Everything" — AI models are already powerful, but the input (context) is the bottleneck that determines the quality of the output. The speaker presented four main ideas to help students use AI more effectively:

- **Activity 1 — Why AI fails when it lacks context**

    + Learn about common mistakes when asking questions to AI: answers that are too general, misdirected, overly verbose, and ignore constraints. The lesson: AI cannot read the mind of users — AI only uses exactly what you provide.

- **Activity 2 — Defining "Context" in AI**

    + Analyze the four components of a quality context: (1) Your goal, (2) Context (student, beginner, group project, deadline), (3) Constraints (tech stack, style, format), (4) Relevant evidence (code, docs, examples).

- **Activity 3 — Three common mistakes when using AI**

    + Mistake #1: "Internet Puller" — dumping entire articles, PDFs, and screenshots into a single chat. Mistake #2: Telling AI things it already knows. Mistake #3: Lack of goals and constraints — resulting in vague prompts and ambiguous responses.

- **Activity 4 — From Prompt → Context → Memory**

    + Learn how AI usage is evolving: from a single question → grounded with documentation → personalized over time. Explore the concept of a "Second AI Brain" and the Store → Retrieve → Generate → Learn process.

- **Activity 5 — Suggesting student-friendly AI projects**

    + The speaker suggested four projects for students to start: AI Study Assistant (summarizing notes, creating quizzes), PDF Chat App (Q&A based on study materials), AI Code Reviewer (explaining bugs, suggesting improvements), and Personal Second Brain (storing and retrieving notes).

### Results and values ​​achieved

- **Lessons learned**

    + Understand that having "many contexts" isn't necessarily better — what matters is a quality context that's focused and relevant to the specific goal.

    + A good prompt must have four elements: Goal, Audience, Constraints, and Success criteria — this is the skill of "communicating" with AI just like communicating with humans.

    + Context engineering will be a core skill in the future, not just prompt engineering.

    + AI is a tool for scaling capabilities — students should start building their own products today instead of just reading theory.

- **Newly Acquired Skills**

    + Technical Skills: Knowledge of designing structured prompts with a Goal — Context — Constraints — Output format. Familiarity with RAG concepts, vector databases, Bedrock, and the Store → Retrieve → Generate → Learn process.

    + Critical Thinking: Ability to distinguish between generic, grounded, and personalized AI responses — thereby evaluating output quality instead of blindly copying and pasting.

    + Personal Knowledge Management (PKM): Understanding how to build a "Second AI Brain" to store notes, documents, and retrieve appropriate context when needed.

    + Soft Skills: Improved ability to ask questions, listen selectively, summarize content, and participate effectively in group discussions.



## 2. Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Quick Suite

A community session organized as part of the AWS Community Day / First Cloud Journey (FCJ) event series. The talk explored Amazon Quick Suite, a unified agentic AI experience that connects to a company's data and world knowledge to help business users gather insights, automate tasks, and accelerate decisions.

### Speakers

- **Pham Ngoc Hai Anh** – G-AsiaPacific Vietnam, AWS Community Builder.

### Summary of content and main activities

The speaker opened by sharing how painful a typical day can be for business users: gathering and analyzing data from multiple sources, repeatedly consulting experts for specialized datasets, and repeating routine time-consuming tasks. The talk then introduced Amazon Quick Suite as AWS's new agentic AI enterprise solution - a unified experience built around an integrated, secure view of company data that simplifies and accelerates the time from data to insight and to action.

- **Activity 1 — Understanding the Pain Points**

    + Explored the daily challenges of business users: gathering information across multiple disconnected sources, relying on experts for specialized datasets, and doing the same repetitive, time-consuming tasks over and over again.

- **Activity 2 — Introduction to Amazon Quick Suite**

    + Learned that Quick Suite offers a unified experience built around an integrated, secure view of company data. It is AWS's new agentic AI offering for the enterprise that simplifies and accelerates the journey from data to decision.

- **Activity 3 — Capabilities Tour (40+ Connectors, Bedrock, Actions)**

    + Walked through Quick Suite's building blocks: 40+ data connectors, user-uploaded files, databases and data warehouses, Bedrock foundation models, web search, thousands of pre-built actions in third-party apps, embeddable UIs and APIs, and responsible AI features (governance, data security, access controls, guardrails, regulatory compliance).

- **Activity 4 — Live Demo**

    + Watched a hands-on demo of how Quick works in practice, including chat-based research, scenario analysis, dashboards, and automation flows for teams across Sales, Marketing, HR and Support.

- **Activity 5 — Use Case: PM Assistant**

    + Explored a concrete use case - a Project Management assistant that can automatically create Minutes of Meeting (MoM), send emails to stakeholders, and schedule the next meeting with minimal manual work.
